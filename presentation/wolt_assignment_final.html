5) Why SCD2 for Item Dimension

Business requirement

  • Item attributes (price, category, name, brand) can change over time.
  • Orders must be evaluated with the item state valid at order time.
  • Without SCD2, historical analyses become inaccurate.

SCD2 behavior

  • Each change creates a new version with valid_from_utc.
  • Previous version closes with valid_to_utc.
  • is_current identifies latest active version.
Defensive fallback kept in pricing join (QUALIFY tie-break) for rare edge cases, even though SCD2 overlap checks are enforced by tests.

7) Incremental Loading Strategy and Watermarks

Where incremental is used

  • Curated log/event-heavy intermediates and selected reporting marts.
  • Key incremental windows use watermark-based lower bound.
  • Lookback is configurable: incremental_lookback_days.

Why this avoids full scans

  • Reads only new/late records after latest processed watermark minus safety window.
  • Prevents expensive full re-read on large log tables each run.
  • Supports reproducible corrective backfill when required.
This design keeps daily runs fast while preserving correctness for late-arriving events.

8) Why Weekly Full/Deep Backfill (30 days) Exists

Problem solved

  • Late events beyond normal lookback may be missed in daily incrementals.
  • Upstream corrections can arrive significantly delayed.

Solution

  • Scheduled deeper reprocessing window (e.g., last 30 days weekly).
  • Captures delayed changes while still cheaper than full-history refresh.
  • Balances cost and data correctness.

Escalation path remains available: full refresh or targeted corrective publish for incidents.

9) Dev and Prod Safety Design

Two-stage orchestration

  • Run dev pipeline first.
  • If dev tests fail, do not trigger prod pipeline.
  • If dev passes, trigger prod run.

Why this matters

  • Protects production from known broken data/model states.
  • Prevents propagation of failing quality checks.
  • Supports confidence in daily automated refreshes.
Dagster is used as the control layer for sequencing and status visibility.

15) Business Findings - Categories (Q1, Q2)

Observed pattern

  • Crisp & Snacks shows strongest broad-based franchise behavior.
  • Chocolate is very strong monetization-wise.
  • Some apparent category movement is a historical category-change effect.

Critical caveat

  • Tony's Brownie appears in two categories over time.
  • Other Confectionary until 2023-08-07.
  • Chocolate from 2023-08-08 onward.
  • This materially affects category trend comparisons.
Interpretation rule: distinguish true demand change from master-data classification shift.

19) Business Findings - Basket & Cross-Sell (Q3)

Framework

  • Support(A,B)
  • Confidence(A→B), Confidence(B→A)
  • Lift(A,B)
  • Expected pair orders under independence

Why expected baseline was added

  • High pair counts can occur purely due to popularity.
  • Expected-vs-actual delta separates real affinity from coincidental overlap.
  • Actionability buckets improve decision quality.
Action focus: bundle high-lift + meaningful-support pairs; avoid substitute-pattern pairs.

  • Trend: is promo acquisition improving or declining month-over-month?

23) Business Findings - Promo Behavior (Q5)

What is measured

  • Share of customers with promo in first order.
  • Share with promo-only first order.
  • Promo-only vs mixed lifetime customer behavior.
  • Promo vs non-promo value and units mix.
  • Monthly promo acquisition rates using first_order_ts_berlin.

Interpretation principle

  • Low promo-first-order share does not mean promos have no value.
  • It means promos are not the only acquisition channel.
  • Cohort follow-up is needed: repeat rate and non-promo conversion after acquisition.

24) Assumptions and Tradeoffs

  • Facts are latest-state unless an explicit snapshot/versioning need exists.
  • Promo rules are assumed to be represented as new records when changed.
  • Berlin business interpretation is preferred over UTC for business trend charts.
  • Incremental pipelines prioritize daily efficiency; weekly deeper backfill handles late-arrival risk.
  • Task 1 SQL dashboard uses core facts directly; Task 2 uses reporting marts for focused business narratives.
